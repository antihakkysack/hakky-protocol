## Suggested visual direction

**Concept:** trust + hygiene, not surveillance. Think "lab-clean" and "verified," not "watchdog." Bright, minimal, high-contrast — the visual opposite of the dark, shadowy aesthetic mixers use (deliberate category signaling).

**Color palette:**
- **Bitcoin Orange** `#F7931A` — accent / provenance nod to BTC (use sparingly, for emphasis and the "clean coin" mark).
- **Clean White** `#FFFFFF` / off-white `#F7F9FA` — dominant background; space and clarity.
- **Trust Teal** `#14B8A6` (or deeper `#0E7C86`) — primary brand color for "verified/clean" states, checkmarks, attestation UI.
- **Ink** `#0B1F2A` — text and dark-mode base; keeps contrast crisp and accessible.
- **Signal amber/red** — reserve for "tainted/expired" states only, never for the brand itself.

**Motifs:**
- A **check/seal** mark (attestation) and a **droplet/soap-bubble** cue for "clean" (the 🧼 emoji is the informal shorthand) — kept geometric and serious, not cartoonish.
- Bitcoin ₿ rendered in teal or with a clean "verified" ring to signal *clean* BTC.
- On-chain / verifiable visual language: subtle hex, signature, or ledger-tick textures — legibility over decoration.

**Do (visual):** lots of whitespace, crisp checkmarks, "verified" green/teal ticks, before/after (tainted→clean) comparisons.
**Don't (visual):** dark hoodie / anonymous / Guy-Fawkes / "hide your tracks" imagery — it invites the exact miscategorization we're fighting.
