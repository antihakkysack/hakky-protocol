## One-line positioning statement

**For honest Bitcoin holders and desks who can't afford a frozen deposit, Hakky Protocol is the clean-Bitcoin layer that turns coin cleanliness into portable, on-chain, verifiable proof — so you can hold and move BTC you can prove is clean. Keep crypto clean.**

## Positioning components (Dunford)

- **Competitive alternatives** — what people do today without Hakky: (a) pay an OTC desk a premium for "clean/virgin" BTC; (b) run opaque, off-chain screening (Chainalysis-style scores) they can't see or carry with them; (c) hope their deposit clears and find out at the withdrawal screen; (d) do nothing and absorb the freeze risk.
- **Unique attributes** — cleanliness is (1) an *asset you hold* (cBTC), not a report you buy; (2) *on-chain and portable*, not a private score locked in one vendor's system; (3) *transparent by design* — provenance is proven, never obscured; (4) paired with a live Proof-of-Reserves oracle; (5) enforced by a policy engine that is *monitor-only by default*.
- **Value** — avoid frozen deposits and refused withdrawals; access the "clean BTC" premium market with proof instead of trust; carry cleanliness across venues instead of re-screening every time.
- **Best-fit customers** — see audiences below.
- **Market category** — "clean / provenance-verified Bitcoin" and "on-chain compliance infrastructure" — deliberately **not** "privacy coin," "mixer," or "anonymity tool." Category choice is the single most important framing decision here.
- **Relevant trend** — rising exchange/regulatory scrutiny of deposit provenance, and growing demand for verifiable (Proof-of-Reserves) rather than trusted backing.

## StoryBrand spine (the narrative we tell)

- **Hero:** the honest BTC holder / desk who wants to move value without landing on a blocklist.
- **Problem:** *External* — deposits freeze, clean BTC costs a premium. *Internal* — anxiety and helplessness; you can't see the risk you're carrying. *Philosophical* — you did nothing wrong; you shouldn't be punished for a coin's past.
- **Guide:** Hakky — empathy ("you shouldn't get frozen for someone else's history") + authority (cBTC, Attestation Registry, PoR oracle).
- **Plan:** hold/redeem cBTC → cleanliness is attested on-chain → verify anytime → move with proof.
- **CTA:** direct = read the whitepaper / star the repo; transitional = follow, read the FAQ threads.
- **Stakes (failure):** a frozen deposit, a refused withdrawal, capital stuck for weeks.
- **Success:** BTC you can prove is clean, accepted anywhere, provenance you carry with you.

1. **Honest BTC holders (retail + HODLers)**
   - Pain: "Could my coins be tainted without me knowing?"
   - Message: cleanliness you can *hold and verify* — stop finding out at the withdrawal screen.

   - Pain: sourcing and proving clean inventory; premium spreads on clean coins.

   - Pain: freezing deposits that trace to tainted coins; support burden and user churn.

4. **Compliance-conscious funds & treasuries**
   - Pain: mandate to avoid sanctioned/illicit exposure; need an audit trail.
   - Message: provenance + Proof-of-Reserves you can show an auditor, with policy that's monitor-only until you decide otherwise.

## Objection handling

**"Is this a mixer / tumbler?"**
No — the exact opposite. Mixers *obscure* where coins came from. Hakky *proves* where they came from. It never mixes, hides, or anonymizes funds; it makes provenance transparent so honest users can demonstrate their coins are clean. If a project's job is to make history unreadable, that's a mixer. Hakky's job is to make history *readable and attested*.

**"Is this a surveillance tool / a way to blocklist people?"**
The Compliance Policy module ships in **monitor-only mode by default** — it observes, it does not block. Screening is *for* cleanliness (helping honest holders prove themselves), not a mechanism we use to freeze anyone. Operators configure their own policy; cleanliness is a tool users hold, not a leash we control.

**"Is it actually trustless / decentralized?"**
Not yet, and we say so plainly. v1 cBTC uses qualified-custodian reserves — the same trust model as every 1:1 BTC-backed token in production today. The roadmap moves to decentralized custody and zk Proof-of-Reserves. We'd rather under-claim than over-promise.

**"Who decides what 'clean' means? Isn't screening subjective?"**
Attestations are issued by *accredited attestors*, signed, and **expiring** (cleanliness is time-bounded, not permanent). Everything is on-chain and checkable, so the basis of an attestation is transparent and auditable rather than a black-box score.

**"Is $HAKKY a token I can buy? Is there a presale?"**
No. $HAKKY is a governance token on the roadmap only — no sale, no airdrop, no price promise. Anyone selling you a "presale" is running a scam. Report it.

**"'Clean BTC' sounds like it enables discrimination against normal users."**
It's opt-in proof for people who *want* to demonstrate cleanliness (desks, funds, cautious holders). It doesn't taint anyone's coins — it gives honest holders a way to carry positive proof they currently lack.

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do**
- Lead with the holder's problem (frozen deposits), not the tech stack.
- Say "screens FOR cleanliness" and "transparent by design" often.
- Be explicit about the v1 custodial trust model and the roadmap.
- Use "verifiable," "portable," "on-chain," "attested," "redeemable 1:1."
- Include "not financial advice / DYOR" on anything that touches value.

**Don't**
- Never imply Hakky hides, mixes, anonymizes, or launders anything.
- Never call it privacy tech, a mixer, or a tumbler — even to contrast.
- No price talk, guaranteed returns, "buy now," APY, or presale language.
- Don't claim it's trustless/decentralized today.
- Don't invent tickers, numbers, partners, or claims beyond the canonical facts.
- Don't punch down at users whose coins are tainted — the enemy is the *invisible, opaque system*, not the holder.

## Tone of voice

Confident, clear, credible, and slightly contrarian. We talk to a crypto-native audience that has been burned by hype, so **honesty is the differentiator**. Plain English over jargon; short declaratives; we name the trade-offs before anyone asks. We are the calm, competent guide — empathy first, then authority (`[[guide-positioning]]`). Never breathless, never salesy, never fear-mongering. When in doubt: under-claim, and show the proof.
